What is PVGO?

A stock's price can be split into two parts: what the company is worth if it never grew again, and how much investors are paying for expected future growth. That second part is the Present Value of Growth Opportunities (PVGO).

No-Growth Value = Forward EPS ÷ Cost of Equity
This is what you'd pay for a bond-like company that just pays out all its earnings forever, with no reinvestment.

PVGO = Current Price − No-Growth Value
The remainder is what the market is paying purely on the belief that the company will find profitable ways to reinvest and grow.

PVGO % tells you how much of the stock price is "growth bet" vs. "earnings value":

  • High PVGO % (e.g. 70–90%) — most of the price is growth expectations. The stock is sensitive to whether those expectations are met.
  • Low or negative PVGO % — the stock trades close to (or below) its no-growth earnings value, suggesting the market sees little growth ahead — or the stock may be undervalued.

The Cost of Equity is estimated with CAPM: Risk-Free Rate + Beta × Market Risk Premium. The risk-free rate is pulled live from the 10-year US Treasury yield (^TNX on Yahoo Finance).
